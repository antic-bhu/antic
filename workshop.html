ABOUT WORKSHOP

The Department of Information Technology at ABV-IIITM, Gwalior proudly presents the 1st International Workshop on Advancing AI and ML Across Disciplines: Use Cases and Emerging Applications (AAMLAD-2025), to be held from December 21‚Äì23, 2025 in Gwalior, India in offline mode (with limited online participation). The accepted and presented papers of this workshop will be published as open-access proceedings in the CCIS series of Springer (Scopus-indexed).

This event offers a vibrant platform for researchers, scientists, and students to present groundbreaking work, engage in technical sessions, and explore the AI and ML Applications across disciplines.

Key Benefits

  • üìÑ Open-access publication opportunities in CCIS series of Springer (SCOPUS Indexed).
  • üåè Global audience & expert networking.
  • üí° Workshops & panel discussions with industry leaders.

We invite the submission of original research papers to this workshop. This workshop seeks to unite pioneering research from academia and industry, highlighting transformative use cases and emerging methodologies in AI and ML. We invite contributions spanning a wide array of AI and ML domains, including but not limited to deep learning, natural language processing, computer vision, reinforcement learning, generative modeling, explainable AI, federated learning, edge AI, cloud/edge computing, quantum computing, and ethical AI. We particularly encourage submissions that demonstrate cross-disciplinary applications in fields such as healthcare, finance, education, agriculture, cybersecurity, robotics, environmental sciences, and the social sciences. By bringing together diverse perspectives, the workshop aims to promote knowledge exchange, foster collaboration, and drive innovation at the intersection of AI, ML, and real-world applications.

SCOPES and TOPICS

  • Advanced deep learning models and optimization strategies
  • Natural language understanding and conversational AI
  • Computer vision, image, and video analysis
  • Reinforcement learning with single and multi-agent systems
  • Generative AI and synthetic data creation
  • Explainable and transparent machine learning
  • Federated and distributed AI frameworks
  • Cloud/Edge computing for AI-enabled devices
  • Responsible and ethical AI practices
  • AI for healthcare diagnostics and medical research
  • Fintech innovations and intelligent trading systems
  • Smart education and adaptive learning platforms
  • AI-powered agriculture and sustainable farming
  • Cybersecurity analytics and automated threat detection
  • Robotics, automation, and autonomous navigation
  • Environmental monitoring and climate prediction
  • Social science research and computational humanities
  • Urban planning, smart cities, and infrastructure optimization

Length of Papers

The most common types of papers accepted for publication are full papers (12‚Äì15+ pages) and short papers (<12 pages), formatted in Springer Template, where a page constitutes 300-400 words. We only wish to publish papers of significant scientific content. Very short papers (<6 pages) are not allowed.

License-to-Publish Form

You will have to send us the signed copy of License-to-Publish form as a scanned PDF, after the acceptance of your manuscript. The corresponding author (one per paper), who must match the corresponding author marked in the header of the paper, must have the full right, power, and authority to sign the agreement on behalf of all of the authors of a particular paper, and accepts responsibility for releasing this material on their behalf. Digital signatures are not acceptable. The corresponding author should be available to check the paper before it is published. Please note that once a paper has been delivered to Springer, changes relating to the authorship of the paper cannot be made. Authors‚Äô names cannot be added or deleted, their order cannot be changed, and the corresponding author cannot be altered. The corresponding author signing the copyright form should match the corresponding author marked on the paper.

Open Access or Open Choice Option

Manuscript Submission & Publication

Abstracting/Indexing of CCIS Springer

The post-proceedings volume published in the Communications in Computer and Information Science (CCIS) series of Springer is abstracted and indexed in major databases including DBLP, Google Scholar, EI-Compendex, Mathematical Reviews, SCImago, and Scopus. CCIS volumes are also submitted for inclusion in ISI Proceedings.

Publication

Authors are first required to submit a one-page abstract. Selected abstracts will be invited for presentation at the conference. After presentation, authors may submit the corresponding full paper for consideration in the post-proceedings volume.

All accepted and registered full papers will be submitted for publication in the Communications in Computer and Information Science (CCIS) series of Springer as an Open Access post-proceedings volume.
Springer CCIS

Before Publication

Only papers that are presented at the conference and subsequently submitted as full papers will undergo a rigorous peer-review process. Based on reviewers‚Äô recommendations and editorial decision, selected papers will be accepted for inclusion in the post-proceedings volume.

After Publication

After publication, authors will receive access to their published papers through SpringerLink. Typically, within a few weeks of publication, authors will be provided with a personal access link enabling them to download the final PDF version of their paper.
